## The 12 mistakes

### Electrical mistakes (these are the expensive ones)

**1. Not checking your electrical panel first.**
Before you buy anything, open your breaker panel and count the open slots. If you don't have room for a 30-60 amp double-pole breaker, you need a panel upgrade. That's $1,500-$3,000. Find this out on day one, not after the sauna is in your driveway.

**2. Running the wrong wire gauge.**
A 6kW heater needs different wiring than a 9kW heater. This is not a "close enough" situation. Wrong wire gauge is a fire hazard. Let your electrician size it based on the exact heater you buy.

**3. Hiring a general handyman for electrical.**
Sauna heaters pull serious amperage. This is licensed electrician territory. Your handy neighbor who "knows electrical" is not the right person for this job.

### Wood and material mistakes

**4. Choosing cedar without considering your climate.**
Western red cedar is the most popular sauna wood. It smells incredible. It also swells and warps in humid climates (Southeast US, Pacific Northwest, coastal areas). If you're in a humid zone, look at thermally treated spruce or aspen instead.

**5. Using the wrong sealant or finish.**
Never use polyurethane, varnish, or paint inside a sauna. At 180F, these off-gas chemicals you do not want to breathe. Use nothing (most traditional approach), or use a sauna-specific paraffin oil treatment.

**6. Skipping the vapor barrier on indoor builds.**
An indoor sauna without a proper vapor barrier will pump moisture into your walls. That's mold in 6-12 months. This is non-negotiable for indoor installations.

### Heater mistakes

**7. Buying too small a heater.**
Heaters are rated by cubic feet. A heater rated for 200 cubic feet will struggle in a 250 cubic foot room. It'll run constantly, wear out faster, and never quite get hot enough. Go one size up from what the math says.

**8. Ignoring stone quality.**
Cheap heater stones crack and crumble within a year. Olivine diabase or peridotite stones last 3-5 years. The cost difference is $30-$50. Not worth cheaping out.

### Placement and planning mistakes

**9. Putting the sauna too far from the house.**
We talked about this yesterday. Distance kills habits. If it takes more than 30 seconds to walk from your door to the sauna, you'll use it less. A lot less.

**10. Forgetting about drainage.**
Traditional saunas produce moisture. Outdoor saunas need ground drainage. Indoor saunas need a floor drain or at minimum a sealed floor that slopes to a drain point. Standing water rots wood fast.

**11. Ignoring ventilation.**
A sauna needs air intake (low, near the heater) and exhaust (high, opposite wall). Without this, the air gets stale, the heat layers unevenly, and the experience suffers. Most cheap kits don't include adequate ventilation.

**12. Not getting permits.**
Yes, outdoor saunas often need permits. Setback requirements, electrical permits, and sometimes building permits. Getting caught without one can mean fines and forced removal. Check with your local building department before you start.
